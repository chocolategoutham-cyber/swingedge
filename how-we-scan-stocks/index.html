Methodology

How we scan stocks

This page explains the market universe, public technical vocabulary, and risk boundaries behind the scanners while keeping exact formulas, thresholds, and weighting proprietary.

Principles

Research candidates, not calls

Scanner rows are research leads. They are not buy, sell, hold, short, or portfolio recommendations.

Multiple clues, not one indicator

Setups are described using structure, trend context, participation, and risk language instead of a single magic signal.

Process stays protected

Definitions are public for clarity; the recipe, weights, thresholds, and ranking rules are not published.

A · Market Universe

What we scan and what we deliberately exclude

Swing Edge is built around a single listed cash-equity venue for reproducible data and clean benchmarking. The research universe is assembled from NSE-listed constituents of Nifty 500, Nifty SmallCap 250, and Nifty MicroCap 250, merged and de-duplicated.

Liquidity-aware gates are applied using participation proxies such as average traded value bands. Exact cutoffs remain proprietary, but the goal is conservative: prefer structures that are easier to observe, backtest, and risk-manage.

Coverage is not limited to F&O listings. If a trader wants only derivatives-eligible names, that check belongs in their own workflow.

B · Technical Logic

How the site talks about price structure

The scanners synthesize structure, trend context, participation, and proximity. Public definitions are shared for clarity while exact windows and thresholds stay private.

Consolidation

A phase where price respects overlapping support and resistance rather than trend-extending.

VCP-like contraction

Tighter swings and smaller pullbacks as a base ages, described in public terms without disclosing exact counting rules.

Relative strength

A way to compare how a stock behaves versus a broad benchmark or peer context.

Breakout proximity

How close price sits to a well-defined pivot or supply shelf where resolution may occur.

Terms on Swing Edge

Public vocabulary, not implementation details

Price structure

Bases, pivots, pullbacks, higher lows, lower highs, and support/resistance areas.

Volume-price behavior

How trading activity behaves around important price zones. Strong moves usually need participation, not only price movement.

Trend alignment

Whether a stock appears to be moving with or against its broader trend context.

Failure risk

Warning signs like false breakouts, overhead supply, weak closes, or support loss.

Scanner Types

Pre-Breakout, Breakout, and Bearish Setup

Pre-Breakout rows gather stocks near potential resolution zones. Breakout rows list names that have already cleared breakout-style levels and now need follow-through review. Bearish Setup rows describe downside structure, support loss, or distribution risk.

Core scanner pages refresh when updated market data and system jobs are available. Context pages can refresh more often when supporting market data is available.

C · Risk Disclaimer

Balanced expectations

  • No scanner guarantees success. A high score or attractive label only means the historical pattern matched research rules at that moment.
  • False signals and failed breakouts are normal. Whipsaws, gaps, news shocks, and correlations can invalidate textbook-looking setups.
  • Risk boundaries still matter. Stops do not eliminate gap risk, but trading without an invalidation level turns research into denial-of-loss behavior.
  • Scanner scores and labels are not buy, sell, or hold recommendations, and past “wins” do not guarantee future results.
